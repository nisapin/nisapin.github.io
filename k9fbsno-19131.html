Is $31 an Hour Good? 5 Interesting Facts to Consider

When it comes to determining whether $31 an hour is a good wage, several factors need to be taken into account. The answer may vary depending on your location, industry, and personal circumstances. To help you make an informed judgment, here are five interesting facts to consider about this hourly wage.

1. Above the National Average Wage:
In the United States, the average hourly wage across all industries is around $27. While $31 an hour may not seem significantly higher, it still exceeds the national average. This suggests that $31 an hour can be considered a good wage in comparison to what many Americans earn.

2. Higher Earning Potential:
An hourly wage of $31 can provide a substantial yearly income, assuming a full-time work schedule of 40 hours per week. With this rate, an individual could potentially earn around $64,480 annually before taxes. This figure places the earner above the national median household income, which was approximately $68,703 in 2019.

3. Varies by Location:
It’s important to note that the cost of living varies greatly across different locations. While $31 an hour may be sufficient in some areas, it may fall short in others. For example, residing in a major city with a high cost of living, such as San Francisco or New York City, could make it more challenging to meet all expenses comfortably. It is crucial to consider the cost of housing, transportation, healthcare, and other living expenses when evaluating the adequacy of an hourly wage.

4. Skill and Education Requirements:
Certain industries and professions command higher wages due to the specialized skills and education they require. For instance, jobs in the medical field, engineering, or technology often offer higher salaries. Therefore, whether $31 an hour is considered good or not depends on the industry. It may be a great wage for someone working in retail but less impressive for a highly skilled professional.
