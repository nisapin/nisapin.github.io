2. Business Ventures and Investments:
Kobe Bryant was not only a basketball player but also a savvy businessman. He founded his venture capital firm, Bryant Stibel, in 2013, along with his business partner Jeff Stibel. The firm specializes in technology, media, and data companies. Bryant Stibel has successfully invested in companies like Alibaba Group, Fortnite creator Epic Games, and LegalZoom, contributing significantly to Kobe’s net worth.

3. Nike’s Lifetime Contract:
Kobe Bryant had a long-standing partnership with Nike, which began in 2003. In 2016, Nike signed a lifetime contract with Kobe, making him the first basketball player to receive such an agreement. The deal secured substantial royalties for Kobe, further boosting his net worth.

5. Philanthropy and Legacy:
Kobe Bryant was known for his philanthropic endeavors. His legacy also lives on through the Mamba Sports Foundation, which aims to provide sports programs and opportunities to underprivileged communities. Kobe’s commitment to giving back not only made a positive impact but also cemented his influential status.
