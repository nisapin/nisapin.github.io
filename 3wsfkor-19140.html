Safe Areas To Live In New Orleans


Safe Areas To Live In New Orleans: 5 Interesting Facts

New Orleans, known for its vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, and lively music scene, is a city that attracts many residents and tourists alike. However, safety is a crucial consideration when choosing a place to live. In this article, we will explore five safe areas to live in New Orleans, along with some interesting facts about each neighborhood.

1. Uptown:

Uptown is one of the safest areas in New Orleans, known for its beautiful historic homes, tree-lined streets, and proximity to Tulane and Loyola Universities. With its friendly atmosphere and strong community bonds, Uptown offers a safe environment for families and young professionals. Magazine Street, a popular shopping and dining destination, runs through the heart of this neighborhood, adding to its charm.

Interesting fact: Uptown is home to Audubon Park, a stunning green space that houses the Audubon Zoo, golf course, and a vast array of oak trees, making it a favorite spot for outdoor activities.

2. Garden District:

The Garden District is another safe and picturesque neighborhood in New Orleans. Known for its well-preserved historic mansions and blooming gardens, this area offers a peaceful and secure living environment. Walking down Magazine Street in the Garden District feels like stepping back in time, with its stunning architecture and wide sidewalks.

Interesting fact: The Garden District is home to Lafayette Cemetery No. 1, one of the city’s most famous above-ground cemeteries. It attracts visitors from all over the world and is known for its unique architecture and tranquil atmosphere.

3. Lakeview:

Situated along the southern shore of Lake Pontchartrain, Lakeview is a predominantly residential neighborhood that offers a safe and serene environment. With its waterfront parks, recreational activities, and beautiful homes, Lakeview is a popular choice for families and outdoor enthusiasts. The neighborhood also boasts excellent public schools, making it an attractive option for parents.

Interesting fact: Lakeview is home to the popular City Park, one of the largest urban parks in the United States. It spans over 1,300 acres and features attractions such as the New Orleans Museum of Art, Botanical Gardens, and the popular Carousel Gardens Amusement Park.

4. Irish Channel:

Located just west of the Garden District, the Irish Channel is a lively and safe neighborhood with a strong sense of community. Known for its Irish heritage, this area offers a mix of historic shotgun houses and renovated cottages. With its vibrant local pubs, delicious cuisine, and annual St. Patrick’s Day parade, the Irish Channel attracts residents who enjoy the city’s rich cultural traditions.

Interesting fact: The Irish Channel is home to the famous St. Alphonsus Church, which hosts the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival’s annual “Jazz Mass.” This unique event combines jazz music with a traditional Catholic mass, creating a memorable experience for locals and visitors alike.

5. Algiers:

Situated across the Mississippi River from downtown New Orleans, Algiers is a safe and peaceful neighborhood that offers a quieter alternative to the bustling city center. With its charming historic architecture, local shops, and access to the riverfront, Algiers provides a tight-knit community feel while still being within easy reach of downtown attractions.

Interesting fact: Algiers is home to the Algiers Ferry, a popular mode of transportation that offers stunning views of the New Orleans skyline. The ferry ride across the Mississippi River is not only a convenient way to access downtown but also a scenic experience in itself.

Common Questions and Answers:

1. Is New Orleans safe to live in?

While New Orleans does have some areas with higher crime rates, there are also many safe neighborhoods to choose from. By doing thorough research and choosing a neighborhood with a low crime rate, you can find a safe place to live in the city.

2. Are there any dangerous neighborhoods to avoid in New Orleans?

Like any metropolitan area, New Orleans has neighborhoods with higher crime rates. It is advisable to avoid areas like Central City, Hollygrove, and the Lower Ninth Ward, which have higher crime rates compared to other parts of the city.

3. Are the touristy areas in New Orleans safe?

The touristy areas of New Orleans, such as the French Quarter and the Garden District, are generally safe during the day. However, it is always wise to stay cautious and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night.

4. Is it safe to walk around New Orleans at night?

It is generally safe to walk around certain parts of New Orleans at night, such as the French Quarter and Uptown. However, it is vital to stay aware of your surroundings, avoid deserted areas, and take precautions like traveling in groups or using well-lit routes.

5. Are there good schools in New Orleans?

Yes, New Orleans has both public and private schools that offer quality education. Some notable public schools include Lusher Charter School, Benjamin Franklin High School, and Lake Forest Elementary Charter School. There are also several private schools with excellent academic reputations.

6. How is the public transportation system in New Orleans?

New Orleans has a public transportation system called the Regional Transit Authority (RTA), which operates buses and streetcars throughout the city. The streetcar lines are particularly popular among tourists and locals alike, providing a unique way to explore the city.

7. What are the average housing prices in safe areas of New Orleans?

The average housing prices in safe areas of New Orleans vary depending on the neighborhood and property type. Generally, Uptown and the Garden District tend to have higher housing costs, while Lakeview and Algiers offer more affordable options.

8. Are there any areas prone to flooding in New Orleans?

Living in a city situated below sea level, there is always a risk of flooding in New Orleans. However, areas like Lakeview, which were heavily affected by Hurricane Katrina, have undergone significant flood mitigation efforts to reduce the risk.

9. How is the job market in New Orleans?

The job market in New Orleans is diverse, with opportunities in various industries such as tourism, healthcare, education, and technology. The city’s growing economy and entrepreneurial spirit make it an attractive destination for job seekers.

10. What are the best neighborhoods for families in New Orleans?

Uptown, Lakeview, and Algiers are among the best neighborhoods for families in New Orleans. These areas offer a safe environment, good schools, and ample recreational activities for children.

11. Is there a high risk of hurricanes in New Orleans?

Yes, safe areas of New Orleans have access to quality healthcare facilities. Ochsner Health System, Touro Infirmary, and Children’s Hospital New Orleans are some of the reputable healthcare providers serving the city.

13. How is the cost of living in safe areas of New Orleans?

The cost of living in safe areas of New Orleans varies, but overall, it is considered affordable compared to other major cities in the United States. Housing, transportation, and food costs are generally lower than the national average.

14. How is the cultural scene in safe areas of New Orleans?

Safe areas of New Orleans, such as Uptown and the Garden District, offer a vibrant cultural scene. From art galleries and museums to live music venues and festivals, residents in these neighborhoods can immerse themselves in the city’s rich cultural heritage.

In conclusion, New Orleans has several safe areas to live in, each offering its unique charm and amenities. Uptown, Garden District, Lakeview, Irish Channel, and Algiers provide residents with secure living environments while showcasing the city’s vibrant culture and history.
